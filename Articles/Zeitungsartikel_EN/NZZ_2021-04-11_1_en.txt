The economy is divided in the CO2 Act The CO2 Act imposes additional burdens on oil imports, road transport and aviation. Directly affected industries are therefore fighting against the law. However, a majority in the business community supports the proposal. The prospect of pots of money, the acceptance of climate targets and concessions to companies are essential reasons. The CO2 Act, revised by the Parliament, which the people voted on in June, provides for dissent in these circles. Economic committees play an active role in both supporters and opponents. The revision of the law is intended to continue climate policy by 2030. The core objective is to halve the Swiss emissions of greenhouse gases by 2030 compared to 1990. The bill contains a mixture of additional environmental taxes, stricter regulations for buildings and vehicles, and an expansion of the subsidy pot called "climate fund". The CO2 levy on fuel oil and natural gas currently amounts to 96 Fr. per tonne; maximum would be 120 Fr. per tonne possible. The revision aims to raise this ceiling to 210 Fr. This is the first time that we have seen a significant increase in the number of tonnes. In addition, the maximum fuel surcharge increases from 5 to 12 Rp. per litre. Furthermore, a new flight billet-discount of 30 to 120 Fr is planned. Per flight.
