Per long-haul flight, 90 francs will be due as a ticket fee CO2 Act Two months before the referendum vote on the revised CO2 Act, the Federal Council provides clarity on the implementation issues. He opened the consultation on the CO2 regulation yesterday. This sets, for example, reduction targets for the various sectors. The revised CO2 Act aims to halve Switzerland's greenhouse gas emissions by 2030 compared to 1990 levels. Switzerland has committed itself to this by signing the Paris Climate Agreement. The CO2 regulation discussed by the Federal Council sets the reduction targets of the individual sectors as follows: In the buildings, the emissions in 2030 are to be reduced by 65 percent compared to 1990, in industry by 35 percent, in transport by 25 percent and in agriculture by 20 percent. As before, the state government is reviewing further measures if the annual greenhouse gas inventory shows that a sector is not on target, the communication continues. The regulation sets out further legal provisions of the CO2 Act. For example, the Federal Council wants to phase out the air ticket tax. For flights in the economy class, a levy of 30 francs for short, 60 francs for medium and 90 francs for long-haul flights is due. Higher travel classes are to be charged with a tax of 30 francs higher each. The regulation was approved on 15 May. July of the current year. (sda)
